Common questions

Things students often ask.

If your question isn't here, just reach out — I'm always happy to chat before you commit to anything.

How long until I can actually speak Hebrew?

Most beginners can hold a basic conversation — introducing themselves, ordering food, asking simple questions — within 2-3 months of weekly lessons. Real fluency takes longer, but you'll feel real progress fast because we focus on practical, modern Hebrew you'll actually use.

I'm a complete beginner. Is that really okay?

It's more than okay — it's exactly who I work with most. The "from zero" in the name is literal. We start with the alphabet, sounds, and the most useful phrases. No prior knowledge, no pressure.

Is online learning really as good as in-person?

In my experience, yes — and often better. We use Zoom or Google Meet, share materials in real time, and you can review recordings. You also get the flexibility of learning from home, no commute, and we can fit your timezone.

What if I need to cancel or reschedule?

Life happens. As long as you give reasonable notice (24 hours when possible), we can reschedule with no issue. I want this to fit your life, not stress you out.

Do I need to learn the Hebrew alphabet first?

No — we'll do it together. I use transliteration (Hebrew written with English letters) at the start so you can speak right away, and we layer in the alphabet gradually so it doesn't feel overwhelming.

What kind of Hebrew will I learn — biblical or modern?

Modern, conversational Hebrew — the kind you'd actually hear on the streets of Tel Aviv or in everyday Israeli life. Not biblical, not academic. Real Hebrew, used by real people today.

How does the free intro call work?

It's a 25-minute Zoom or Google Meet call — completely free, no card required. We'll talk about your goals, I'll get a sense of where you're starting from, and you'll see if my teaching style fits you. Zero pressure to book lessons after.
